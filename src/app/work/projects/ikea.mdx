## Overview

IKEA is a multinational conglomerate that develops and produces quick-to-assemble furniture, kitchen appliances, and home accessories. IKEA was looking to create a social campaign targeting children (5-10yo), focused on environmental awareness while simultaneously promoting IKEA’s plush animals.

We came forward with an idea for a mobile quiz game. Gamified learning proved to be more memorable and easier to internalise than traditional classes and presentations. 

## Logo and Title

“Hej! Keep it simple.” is a slogan of the corporate culture of the IKEA company. It’s friendly and casual. We decided to tag along and call the app "Hej! Eko". 
My goal was to relay the mood and feel of the applications, as well as to make it attractive to kids and good-looking as an icon on a smartphone’s screen.

## Sketches

![in-game screens](/images/projects/project-01/ikea_inline_4.png)

We created a ‘buddy’ companion following the player throughout the game. The player can choose their ‘buddy’ from 5 different characters. Each one had their own personality and a unique set of animations, designed to keep the kids engaged with learning.
These characters personify recycled things, which reinforces the main subject of the app. All characters are gender neutral.

![in-game screens](/images/projects/project-01/ikea_inline_2.png)

UMCS’ Psychology Department took active part in the preproduction stage and supplied studies revolving around kids with learning difficulties. It help us with inventing the game mechanics and presenting information in a kid-friendly format.
I also created illustrations with the most popular IKEA toys - the rewards for winning the game.

## Challenges and Learnings

One of the biggest challenges was ensuring that the generated code was clean and maintainable. This involved setting up intelligent mapping between Figma components and React code structures, as well as managing edge cases like responsive design and conditional rendering. Additionally, the continuous synchronization required a robust error-handling system to prevent conflicts during development.
